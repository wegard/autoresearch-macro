### Major comments

1. **The headline claim (ÔÇťimprove forecasts for small open economiesÔÇŁ) is broader than the evidence, and the core contribution needs sharper positioning.**  
   The empirical evidence is a single-country case study with four target series. ţłÇfileciteţłéturn0file0ţłü That can still be publishable in the IJFÔÇöparticularly if it delivers a clear methodological lesson for practitionersÔÇöbut the paper should be reframed as (i) a careful pseudo-real-time evaluation of a specific TS foundation model plus (ii) an empirical demonstration that automated pipeline search can overfit macro regimes even when it selects economically sensible covariates. As written, the title and introduction suggest external validity across ÔÇťsmall open economies,ÔÇŁ but the design does not support that generalization without either additional countries or stronger validation that the mechanism is generic (e.g., showing similar overfitting patterns under alternative regime splits or alternative commodity exporters). ţłÇfileciteţłéturn0file0ţłü

2. **Benchmark set is not adequate for IJF macro forecasting standards, and the current baselines create a fairness problem.**  
   The classical baselines are all essentially univariate (random walk, seasonal naive, AR/ARIMA, ETS). ţłÇfileciteţłéturn0file0ţłü Yet the agent-tuned Chronos-2 configuration explicitly uses multiple covariates discovered by the search. ţłÇfileciteţłéturn0file0ţłü This makes the comparison structurally asymmetric: you are effectively comparing a multivariate (or at least covariate-augmented) model class to purely univariate benchmarks. For macroeconomic forecasting in the IJF, credible competing baselines should include data-rich and multivariate approaches that operationalize the classic ÔÇťlarge information setÔÇŁ paradigm associated with ţłÇentityţłé["people","James H. Stock","economist time series"]ţłü and ţłÇentityţłé["people","Mark W. Watson","economist time series"]ţłü (diffusion-index / factor models), as well as at least one shrinkage multivariate benchmark (e.g., BVAR with Minnesota-type priors) and a modern ML benchmark that is standard in the macro ML literature (e.g., penalized regression / random forest / boosting with time-series-appropriate validation). Without these, the empirical relevance for practitioners is not convincingly established, and readers cannot tell whether the main ÔÇťoverfittingÔÇŁ phenomenon is specific to TSFMs/agentic search or simply a generic pitfall of covariate selection in small samples.

3. **Forecast evaluation lacks statistical inference and does not address multiple-horizon dependence or multiple-testing/data-snooping.**  
   The paper reports average RMSE tables and narrates differences (e.g., ÔÇťbeats ARIMA at horizons ÔÇŽÔÇŁ). ţłÇfileciteţłéturn0file0ţłü For IJF, this is not enough: forecast comparisons should be accompanied by formal tests, at least variants of the DieboldÔÇôMariano framework associated with ţłÇentityţłé["people","Francis X. Diebold","economist forecasting"]ţłü and ţłÇentityţłé["people","Roberto S. Mariano","economist forecasting"]ţłü, with careful attention to overlapping multi-step forecast errors (h = 3, 6, 12) and serial correlation in loss differentials. Beyond pairwise testing, the agentic search itself constitutes a model-selection procedure over (at minimum) covariate sets, transforms, context lengths, and fine-tuning settings across 50 iterations. ţłÇfileciteţłéturn0file0ţłü This creates classic ÔÇťdata snoopingÔÇŁ concerns: the reported 6.8% validation improvement is the result of adaptive search on the same validation sample, so its nominal magnitude is not interpretable without adjustment. At minimum, the revision should (i) formalize the selection framework and (ii) report inference that accounts for search (e.g., reality-check / SPA-style adjustments or nested evaluation), otherwise the key quantitative claims about ÔÇťimprovementÔÇŁ are not statistically grounded.

4. **The validation/test split is clean but too brittle; the paper needs an evaluation design that tests whether ÔÇťagentic searchÔÇŁ adds value in a realistic sequential workflow.**  
   The current design chooses a single validation era (2006ÔÇô2015) for search and then freezes the pipeline for 2016ÔÇô2025. ţłÇfileciteţłéturn0file0ţłü This is a legitimate first pass, but it answers a very specific question: ÔÇťDoes a pipeline selected on a single pre-2016 macro regime generalize across later structural breaks?ÔÇŁ The answer is ÔÇťoften no,ÔÇŁ which is interesting. ţłÇfileciteţłéturn0file0ţłü However, practitioners rarely freeze all pipeline choices for a decade in a changing economy if re-estimation or re-tuning is possible. The revision should add at least one realistic sequential alternative, such as: (i) re-running a constrained search at a low frequency (e.g., annually) using only information available at each re-tuning date; or (ii) a rolling/expanding validation that explicitly includes multiple regimes in the selection objective; or (iii) an ensemble strategy combining zero-shot and tuned configurations with weights updated in pseudo-real-time. Without at least one such design, the practical guidance risks being read as ÔÇťdonÔÇÖt tune,ÔÇŁ whereas the more actionable message may be ÔÇťtune, but tune with regime-robust objectives and explicit controls against selection overfit.ÔÇŁ

5. **The ÔÇťagenticÔÇŁ component is not isolated; the paper needs a non-LLM search baseline to justify the use of an LLM as a meta-optimizer.**  
   The search space is a finite combinatorial space (subset of 14 covariates, discrete context lengths, discrete fine-tuning step counts, a learning-rate grid, and a limited set of transforms). ţłÇfileciteţłéturn0file0ţłü It is entirely plausible that random search, Bayesian optimization, or successive halving could find similar configurations under the same evaluation budgetÔÇöespecially since the accepted improvements look like incremental covariate additions plus context truncation. ţłÇfileciteţłéturn0file0ţłü As written, the reader cannot tell whether the LLM provides (i) sample efficiency, (ii) interpretability, or (iii) any systematic advantage over standard AutoML approaches. A revision should include at least one search baseline matched on compute budget and evaluation calls (e.g., random search with the same number of full evaluations; Bayesian optimization with the same budget). If the core contribution is that ÔÇťagentic search is promising but fragile,ÔÇŁ then the appropriate comparison is: fragile relative to what?

6. **Key implementation details required for replication are missing or under-specified, particularly around the LLM and foundation-model interfaces.**  
   The paper states that the agent uses ÔÇťClaude SonnetÔÇŁ via the API and reads a specification file (ÔÇťprogram.mdÔÇŁ), proposing JSON diffs. ţłÇfileciteţłéturn0file0ţłü For reproducibility, the revision must precisely document: exact model identifier/version and date, prompt text and system instructions, sampling parameters (temperature, top-p), tool/function-call schemas if used, how iterations are logged, and whether any human intervention occurred (e.g., manual reruns, cherry-picked seeds). The same applies to Chronos-2 via the AutoGluon interface: the exact checkpoint, library versions, default preprocessing, treatment of missing values/ragged edge, and how covariates are encoded (past-only vs known-future). ţłÇfileciteţłéturn0file0ţłü In macro forecasting, small implementation details can dominate results; without a complete computational appendix and public code, the study is not replicable.

7. **The paperÔÇÖs primary metric and aggregation choices are currently confusing and risk misinterpretation.**  
   The search objective is ÔÇťaverage MASE across all four targets and four horizons,ÔÇŁ where MASE is defined using a random-walk MAE denominator. ţłÇfileciteţłéturn0file0ţłü Yet the reported validation MASE values are far above 1 (baseline 1.9443, best 1.8129). ţłÇfileciteţłéturn0file0ţłü Under the paperÔÇÖs own definition (ÔÇťvalues below 1 indicate improvement over the naive baselineÔÇŁ), these values imply that the evaluated Chronos configurations are substantially worse than the random walk on the validation objective as aggregated, even after ÔÇťimprovement.ÔÇŁ ţłÇfileciteţłéturn0file0ţłü This sharply changes the practical interpretation: a 6.8% improvement may still correspond to forecasts that practitioners would not use. The revision must (i) clarify exactly how MASE is computed (per-series then averaged vs pooled; per-horizon scaling vs a common denominator; treatment of missing outcomes), (ii) report MASE for all baselines alongside RMSE, and (iii) emphasize relative performance to naive benchmarks in a way that is interpretable for practitioners (e.g., relative RMSE/MAE vs random walk by series and horizon).

8. **The ÔÇťoverfittingÔÇŁ diagnosis is plausible but not yet established as a mechanism specific to agentic search rather than to the fine-tuning schedule, covariate leakage, or macro nonstationarity.**  
   The paper attributes deterioration primarily to covariate relationships breaking during regime change. ţłÇfileciteţłéturn0file0ţłü That is plausible, but the current evidence is mostly descriptive (subperiod RMSE tables). ţłÇfileciteţłéturn0file0ţłü Several alternative explanations are not ruled out:  
   - The fine-tuning choice is ÔÇťfit once at the first origin, reuse forever,ÔÇŁ which the paper itself flags as potentially overfitting early-period patterns. ţłÇfileciteţłéturn0file0ţłü This could mechanically worsen post-2022 performance independent of the covariate set.  
   - The handling of quarterly predictors by forward-fill and daily-to-monthly aggregation could change information content in ways that interact with shocks; without sensitivity checks (e.g., dropping quarterly predictors, using end-of-month rather than average, alternative release-lag assumptions) it is hard to attribute differences to covariate ÔÇťrelationshipsÔÇŁ rather than data engineering choices. ţłÇfileciteţłéturn0file0ţłü  
   - The agent ÔÇťlearnsÔÇŁ that transforms do not help, but this may reflect the modelÔÇÖs implicit scaling/tokenization rather than economic structure; this should be probed with controlled ablations. ţłÇfileciteţłéturn0file0ţłü  
   A revision should provide mechanism-focused ablations: keep covariates fixed but vary fine-tuning schedule; keep fine-tuning fixed but vary covariate set; assess whether the failure is concentrated in specific targets/horizons (the manuscript currently has TODO placeholders for per-variable tables). ţłÇfileciteţłéturn0file0ţłü

### Overall assessment

**Recommendation: major revision.**

The paper addresses a timely and practically relevant questionÔÇöwhether time-series foundation models can be improved for macro forecasting by automated, LLM-guided pipeline selectionÔÇöand it uses a pseudo-real-time discipline with explicit publication lags, which aligns with IJF expectations. ţłÇfileciteţłéturn0file0ţłü The negative out-of-sample result (agent-selected improvements fail under later structural breaks while zero-shot performance is relatively more robust) is potentially valuable to practitioners precisely because it cautions against na├»ve AutoML-style tuning in nonstationary macro environments. ţłÇfileciteţłéturn0file0ţłü

At present, however, the manuscript does not meet the evidentiary bar for IJF due to major gaps in benchmarking, statistical evaluation, and reproducibility. The baseline set is not macro-standard and is not symmetric with respect to covariate usage; the search procedure is not compared to non-LLM optimization; forecast comparisons lack formal inference and do not address data-snooping induced by adaptive search; and key implementation details (LLM configuration, exact prompts, Chronos-2 interface specifics, missing-data handling) are not fully documented. ţłÇfileciteţłéturn0file0ţłü Several TODO placeholders also indicate the paper is not yet submission-ready. ţłÇfileciteţłéturn0file0ţłü
