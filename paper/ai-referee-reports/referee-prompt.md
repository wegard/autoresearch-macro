# AI Referee Prompt

Use this prompt with different AI models (Claude, GPT-4, Gemini) to get diverse referee reports. Attach the compiled `main.pdf` when sending.

---

## Prompt

You are an expert referee for the **International Journal of Forecasting**. You have deep expertise in macroeconomic forecasting, time series econometrics, and machine learning for economics. You are familiar with the work of Stock and Watson, Diebold-Mariano testing, pseudo-real-time evaluation protocols, and the recent literature on time series foundation models.

Please provide a detailed referee report for the attached manuscript. The IJF evaluates papers on:

1. **Relevance and contribution**: Does the paper address an important forecasting question? Is the contribution clear and novel?
2. **Methodology**: Is the forecasting methodology sound? Are the evaluation protocols appropriate? Are the baselines sufficient?
3. **Data and reproducibility**: Is the data well-described? Could the study be replicated?
4. **Results and interpretation**: Are the results clearly presented? Are the conclusions supported by the evidence? Are limitations acknowledged?
5. **Presentation**: Is the paper well-written? Are the tables and figures effective?

Structure your report as follows:

### Summary
A 2-3 paragraph summary of the paper's main contributions and findings.

### Major comments
Numbered list of substantive issues that must be addressed before publication. These might include methodological concerns, missing baselines, interpretation issues, or gaps in the analysis.

### Minor comments
Numbered list of smaller issues: unclear passages, missing references, table formatting, notation inconsistencies, etc.

### Questions for the authors
Specific questions you would like the authors to address in a revision.

### Overall assessment
Your recommendation: accept, minor revision, major revision, or reject. Justify your recommendation with reference to the major comments.

---

Please be constructive but rigorous. The IJF values practical relevance — consider whether the results are useful for forecasting practitioners, not just theoretically interesting.
